Malala Yousafzai

Malala Yousafzai is a Pakistani activist for women's education and the youngest Nobel Prize laureate. She gained international attention in 2012 after surviving an assassination attempt by the Taliban for her advocacy of girls' education. Malala founded the Malala Fund, which works to secure education for girls in underprivileged communities. She has authored two books, including the international bestseller "I Am Malala: The Story of the Girl Who Stood Up for Education and Was Shot by the Taliban."

Malala continues to be a powerful voice for human rights and education, and has been recognized with numerous awards and honors for her activism.
